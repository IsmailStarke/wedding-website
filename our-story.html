Our story began in different corners of South Africa. The Groom, coming from the Cape Town, while The Bride, from in Felixton, nestled in KZN. From the moment we met during the orientation week on University, fate had its way as our paths crossed unexpectedly at the University of Pretoria. It felt as though Allah (God) had brought us together. After mulling about it many times, we realised that there were so many ways in which we wouldn't meet.

However, our journey wasn't without its challenges. Once we became a couple, summoning the courage to tell our parents about our relationship was terrifying. Meeting each other's families was even worse, yet on both sides we were accepted. And we are immesenly grateful to them for everything they have done for us thus far.

For nearly five years, we stood firm in fighting for our relationship, weathering various storms. The pandemic was the worst. Having to endure an 8-hour distance between us for many months, adding to the strain. Holidays became bittersweet when she had to return home, separating us once more.

Yet, despite the trials - enduring long-distance, the high-stress of university, and confronting various other obstacles - here we stand, on the brink of marriage.
